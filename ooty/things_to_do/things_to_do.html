1.Enjoy a Fun Railway Ride in the Nilgiri Mountain Toy Train

Get ready to experience the most fun and joyful railway ride of your life with the Nilgiri Mountain Toy Train . Offering a pleasant view of the lush green hills, picturesque valleys, forested landscapes, and verdant meadows from the window seat, the Nilgiri Mountain Toy Train, commonly known as Ooty Toy Train, invites people to witness the sheer beauty of the Nilgiris in its own way.

It is the best mode of transport to travel from Mettupalayam to Ooty and presents its tourists with a kind of experience that is hard to get anywhere else.

Popular among holidaymakers of all ages, especially children, the Ooty Toy Train passes through 16 tunnels, 208 steep curves, and 250 beautiful bridges to cover a total distance of approximately 46 Km in 5 hours.

Built in 1908 by the Britishers, this century-old UNESCO World Heritage Site takes you through the best of nature with a glimpse of lovely mountain ranges and picture-worthy sites.

The overall scenery you get to see on this spectacular journey is simply breathtaking! And the best part is, it makes you forget everything about the destination as you go up from 300 feet to 7200 feet, thus giving you a chance to travel altogether with a different feeling.

  • Entry Fee: INR 205 per person for first class, INR 30 per person for second class, and INR 15 for unreserved category.
  • Timings: The departure time of the train from Mettupalayam to Ooty is at 7:10 AM every day and from Ooty to Mettupalayam, it is at 2:00 PM every day.
  • Distance: 2 Km from Ooty Centre
  • Exploration Time: 5-6 Hours

2. Admire the Natural Beauty and Cascading Waterfalls at Dolphin’s Nose

Witness the lush green tea estates and the delightful view of the mountains from the hilltop location at Dolphin’s Nose. Nestled amidst the rolling hills, the Dolphin’s Nose creates a picture-perfect setting for a much-needed getaway and offers a jaw-dropping view of the Catherine Falls .

A preferable destination for tourists to soak in the serenity and admire the surrounding beauty, Dolphin’s Nose catches the attention of people from all walks of life. It is a delightful treat for nature buffs and is no less than a heaven for adventure junkies as well.

The blue mountain ranges and the spectacular landscapes are hard to miss here and the fresh breeze blowing your hair lets you relax soulfully, far away from the chaotic life. Unlike other tourist places to visit in Ooty , this famous attraction remains less crowded and is bestowed with excellent weather conditions so you can have a fun-filled day out with your family.

Walk hand-in-hand with your loved ones to overlook the 180-degree view of the adjoining scenery. Here you can also watch the sunrise or sunset to soothe your eyes with breathtaking scenery. Positioned at an altitude of 1,550 meters above sea level, this attraction is just a stroll away from Lady Canning’s Seat and has also become an ideal picnic spot for locals as well.

  • Entry Fee: INR 15 per person
  • Timings: Open from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM on all days
  • Distance: 12 Km from Coonoor, 10 Km from Coonoor Railway Station, 24 Km from Ooty
  • Exploration Time: 1 Hour

3. Discover the Exotic Flavours of Tea at the Tea Museum

Treat yourself to an exciting weekend getaway at Tea Museum. Also referred to as Ooty Tea Factory, the Tea Museum is a famous scenic point for all interested in exploring the amazing tea garden on their Ooty tour and is indeed a must-visit destination for tea lovers.

Situated adjacent to the Doddabetta Tea Factory, the Ooty Tea Museum boasts of an excellent location with lush-green tea plantations encircling the area. It’s a place where you can learn everything about the evolution of tea from origin to the present. It’s a place where you can indulge yourself in the aroma of fresh tea leaves and see how it is being brewed. It’s a place where you can see how your everyday morning cup of tea is prepared.

The magnificent Nilgiri mountains take you one step closer to nature and the emerald tea fields please your senses with enchanting views. Owing to its surreal setting and pleasant atmosphere, the museum draws thousands of people from everywhere and has become the best attraction for wanderers as well.

When you leave this place, you’ll definitely fall in love with tea and would want to sip it for a lifetime. Don’t forget to pay a visit to the in-house souvenir shop and buy a diverse range of tea back home. Other than that, the shop also sells a variety of teacups and tea plates at pocket-friendly prices.

  • Entry Fee: INR 250 per person
  • Timings: Open from 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM on all days
  • Distance: 5.5 Km from Ooty Centre and Ooty Railway Station
  • Exploration Time: 1 Hour

4. Walkthrough the Ooty Botanical Gardens

Be sure to take some time off for the Botanical Gardens in Ooty when you’re on your Ooty tour. Home to 650 plus species of different flowers and trees, the Government Botanical Garden is a well-maintained, well-organized garden where you can enjoy astounding colorful natural beauty at its best along with overlooking the verdant landscapes at its backdrop.

Perfect for sightseeing lovers, fun seekers, and photographers, the Government Botanical Garden invites people to have a fun-filled day out amid lush greenery. Situated on the slope of the famous Doddabetta Peak , the garden serves as an idyllic destination for all who fancy spending some time away from the hustle-bustle of everyday life. It is a great place to discover a rare species of exotic plants and is definitely a go-to place for a half-day excursion.

Sprawling across 55 hectares of land, the Government Botanical Garden is mainly divided into 5 sections named as a Lower Garden, New Garden, Italian Garden, The Conservatory, and the Nurseries. It boasts an array of spectacular lawns, exotic woods, man-made ponds with lilies, orchids, flowering plants of over 50,000 varieties, and several medicinal herbs.

Other highlights of this park include a fossil tree that is supposed to be 20 million years old and Toda Hill that gives a glimpse of the lifestyle and culture of the Toda tribe.

Another point of interest of the Ooty Botanical Garden is its flower show which is successfully organized as a part of the summer festival of Ooty in May month every year. It is a 2-day affair that showcases a magnificent collection of flowering plants, thus attracting approximately 1,50,000 tourists from different corners of the world. So, don’t miss it if you happen to visit this romantic hill station during that time.

  • Entry Fee: INR 30 per person for adults, INR 15 per person for children
  • Timings: Open from 7:00 AM to 6:30 PM on all days
  • Distance: 2.5 Km from Ooty
  • Exploration Time: 2-3 Hours

5. Enjoy Safari at Mudumalai National Park

Visiting the Mudumalai National Park for a safari ride is undoubtedly the highlight of everyone’s trip to Ooty. With so much to see and do throughout the day, it is a place where you can leverage the benefits of the world’s best safari with unexpected twists and turns.

One can choose from Bus Safari, Jeep Safari, or an elephant safari to explore the surrounding area of the wildlife reserve. You can also opt for the nighttime safari tour to add more adventure to your visit. All the options are equally fascinating and rewarding. So, you don’t have to worry about anything.

The most sought-after tourist destination happens to be on the north-western side of the Nilgiri mountains, Mudumalai National Park or Mudumalai Tiger Reserve boasts more than 500 different species of endangered creatures including animals, birds, reptiles, mammals, amphibians, and thousands of insects. In addition to this, you also get to see majestic Elephants, Deer, and Langurs roaming freely inside the park.

Famous for its tiger population, the wildlife reserve is considered as the popular wildlife habitat in South India that provides breathtaking and captivating scenery for anyone who appreciates nature. It is a place where you can soak in the gorgeous greenery and uncover a variety of hidden forests on your way.

A must-visit tourist destination for wildlife lovers, the Mudumalai National Park promises to deliver the best safari experience to all its tourists and allows you to be one step closer to nature.

  • Entry Fee: INR 30 per person; however, there will be an extra charge for a safari ride.
  • Timings: Open from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM on all days
  • Distance: 29 Km from Ooty Centre
  • Exploration Time: 4-5 Hours
